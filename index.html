About Me

A biotechnologist turned information professional, I am a Ph.D. Candidate at the Department of Library and Information Science, University of Delhi, India. I am also serving as the Editor-in-Chief of the International Journal of Library and Information Services (IJLIS) published by IGI Global and Newsletter Officer for ASIS&T South Asia Chapter.

My PhD thesis, “Analyzing Trends in Electronic Theses and Dissertations 100 years of Library and Information Science”, is the first doctoral thesis in India that focuses on the application of computational methods in the field of library and information science (LIS). As the number of textual data, including ETDs, increases exponentially every day over the web, the issue of organizing, managing, and disseminating information poses a challenge. My PhD research aims to forward a solution to the above problem by applying topic modeling, network analysis, and machine learning to the ETDs submitted to the ProQuest Dissertations and Theses (PQDT) Global database from 1903 to 2020. I‚Äôm particularly interested to identify the highly-and-under researched areas in LIS over 100 years and understand the evolution of LIS discipline in different countries.

‚ÄãIn June 2020, I was selected to attend a two-week research-based summer-program in computational social science (SICSS 2020) that covered automated text analysis, website scraping, digital field experiments, non-probability sampling, mass collaboration, ethics, machine learning, network analysis, and many other issues. Currently, I am working on five different research projects besides my doctoral research.
